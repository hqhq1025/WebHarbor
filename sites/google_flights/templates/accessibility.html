Accessibility statement

Google Flights is designed to be usable by everyone, including travelers who rely on assistive technology such as screen readers (VoiceOver, NVDA, TalkBack), keyboard-only navigation, and high-contrast modes. This page summarises the practices and conformance level our flight search interface targets.

Conformance target

We aim to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. The list below documents the accessibility patterns shipped on every page. Each item is verifiable on the homepage and flight detail pages.

Screen-reader notes

Flight result cards expose route, airline, departure time, duration, stops, and price as a single accessible label that VoiceOver reads in one swipe. Filter chips are buttons (not links) so screen readers announce them as toggle controls. Sort selectors are native <select> elements so VoiceOver rotor navigation works without custom ARIA scaffolding.
