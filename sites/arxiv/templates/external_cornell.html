Overview

Cornell University is a private Ivy League research university located in Ithaca, New York. Founded in 1865 by Ezra Cornell and Andrew Dickson White, it was chartered as a co-educational and non-sectarian institution. Cornell is one of the two private land-grant universities in the United States.

  • College of Architecture, Art, and Planning (AAP)
  • College of Arts and Sciences
  • Cornell SC Johnson College of Business
  • College of Engineering
  • College of Human Ecology
  • School of Industrial and Labor Relations (ILR)

Graduate and professional schools include:

  • Cornell Law School
  • Weill Cornell Medicine (in New York City and Doha)
  • Cornell University Graduate School
  • Cornell Tech (in New York City)
  • College of Veterinary Medicine

Research and arXiv

Cornell University is the host institution for arXiv.org, the world's largest open-access archive for scholarly preprints. arXiv was founded in 1991 by Paul Ginsparg and has been maintained at Cornell since 2001. The archive is operated by Cornell University Library with support from the Simons Foundation, member institutions, and individual donors.

Cornell's broader research enterprise is among the largest in the United States, with research expenditures exceeding US$1.2 billion per year across life sciences, engineering, humanities, social sciences, and agriculture.
