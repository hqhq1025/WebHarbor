Privacy Notice

Last updated: 4 March 2026.

This notice explains how this BBC News mirror handles the personal information you share with it when you visit, register an account, or use any of the features described below. It mirrors the public-facing language of the BBC's own privacy notice, adapted for a learning environment that runs entirely on a single local host.

What we collect

  • Account fields you give us at sign-up: email, username, full name, optional bio and location.
  • Activity data generated by you on the site: reading history, bookmarks, reading-list folders, subscriptions, digests and comments.
  • Technical session data: signed-in session cookie, CSRF token, and request log lines kept in memory for diagnostic purposes only.

How we use it

Your data is used solely to power the features you see on the site — your reading history is yours, the bookmark page only shows your own bookmarks, and digests are only ever sent to the email you set on your own account. We do not run advertising, and we do not share anything with a third party.

Cookies

We set a small number of strictly-necessary cookies, described in detail on the Cookies page. There are no analytics or advertising cookies on this mirror.

Your rights

You can edit your account at any time from the My Account page, clear your reading history from the History page, and delete your account from the account settings. Account deletion permanently removes your comments, bookmarks, reading-list items, subscriptions and digests.

Contact

Questions about this privacy notice can be sent via the Contact page.
