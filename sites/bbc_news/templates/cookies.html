Cookies on BBC News

Last updated: 4 March 2026.

Cookies are small text files that the site asks your browser to store. We use a small number of them to keep the site working. There are no advertising or third-party analytics cookies on this mirror.

Strictly necessary cookies

  • session — keeps you signed in across page loads. Expires when you sign out or close your browser.
  • csrf_token — protects form submissions from cross-site request forgery. Expires with the session.
  • nav_state — remembers which side-navigation groups you have expanded. Optional; the site works without it.

Functional cookies

  • reading_pref — remembers your preferred font size and theme between visits. Lifetime: 1 year. Cleared when you log out.

Analytics and advertising

None are set on this mirror. Tasks that ask you to "accept all cookies" or to dismiss a tracking banner do not apply here.

Managing cookies

You can clear or block these cookies through your browser's settings. Doing so may sign you out and reset your reading preferences, but will not affect your stored account data.
