About the BBC

The British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) is the world's leading public service broadcaster. Our mission, set out by Royal Charter, is to act in the public interest, serving all audiences through the provision of impartial, high-quality and distinctive output and services which inform, educate and entertain.

What we do

  • Provide impartial news and information to help people understand and engage with the world around them.
  • Support learning for people of all ages.
  • Show the most creative, highest quality and distinctive output and services.
  • Reflect, represent and serve the diverse communities of all of the United Kingdom's nations and regions.
  • Reflect the United Kingdom, its culture and values to the world.

News, in numbers

BBC News reaches a global audience of nearly half a billion people each week, broadcasting in more than 40 languages across television, radio, online and mobile services. The newsroom in Broadcasting House, London, runs 24 hours a day alongside major UK bureaux in Glasgow, Cardiff and Belfast and over 50 international bureaux from Washington to Singapore.

Editorial values

All BBC content is produced according to the BBC's Editorial Guidelines, our editorial code of conduct. The guidelines apply to every output across BBC News, Sport, iPlayer, Sounds, the World Service and our online services. They are built around the values of trust, truth and accuracy, impartiality, editorial integrity and independence, harm and offence, serving the public interest, fairness, privacy, and accountability.

Funded by the licence fee

The BBC is principally funded through the licence fee paid by UK households. Income from the licence fee is supplemented by commercial revenue from BBC Studios, the BBC's commercial arm, which is reinvested into programmes and services for audiences in the UK and around the world.
