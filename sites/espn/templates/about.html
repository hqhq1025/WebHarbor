About ESPN

ESPN, The Worldwide Leader in Sports, serves fans through ESPN.com, ESPN+, eight U.S. television networks, multiple radio properties, and digital platforms.

Our Mission

To serve sports fans. Anytime. Anywhere. Through the connection of editorial, technology, and a deep portfolio of live rights, ESPN is the destination for sports.

By the Numbers

  • 1979 — Founded in Bristol, Connecticut
  • 200+ Countries and territories that receive ESPN content
  • 8 Linear U.S. networks (ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNU, ESPNEWS, ESPN Deportes, ESPN Classic, SEC Network, ACC Network)
  • 4,000+ Live events streamed annually on ESPN+

Leadership

ESPN is part of The Walt Disney Company. The company is led by Chairman Jimmy Pitaro, who oversees ESPN's editorial direction, business operations, and global growth strategy.

Corporate Responsibility

The V Foundation for Cancer Research, founded by ESPN and the late Jim Valvano in 1993, has raised more than $310 million to fund cancer research grants. ESPN also operates Coaches vs. Cancer, the Jimmy V Classic, and Saturday Night at the V.

Editorial Standards

ESPN journalists adhere to a published editorial policy that covers sourcing, conflict-of-interest disclosure, and corrections. The full Editorial Standards document is available in our press room.
