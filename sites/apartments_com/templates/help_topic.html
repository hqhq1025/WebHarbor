Common questions

{{ title }} — most asked question

Detailed answer for the most common question on this topic. Renters working through this stage typically encounter a few specific pain points; we cover them step-by-step below.

How long does this stage take?

It depends on the market and your preparation. With docs ready, most renters complete this in 3–5 business days.

What documents do I need?

Photo ID, two most recent pay stubs (or 2 years of tax returns if self-employed), bank statements, and references for any prior landlords.

Can I do this with bad credit?

Yes — landlords accept letters of explanation, larger deposits, co-signers, or guarantor-service alternatives.

Step-by-step checklist

  1. Identify your target neighborhoods and budget.
  2. Gather application documents.
  3. Apply within 48 hours of touring — units move fast.
  4. Negotiate concessions if the market is soft.
  5. Read the lease before signing — flag anything unclear.
