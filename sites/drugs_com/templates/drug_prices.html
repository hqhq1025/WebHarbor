What affects the price?

Generic vs. brand name

Generic versions of the same medication typically cost 80-90% less than the brand-name product while delivering the same active ingredient and clinical effect.

Insurance coverage

Copays vary widely by plan tier. Some plans cover generics with a $0-$10 copay, while non-preferred brand drugs may require co-insurance of 25-50%.

Quantity discounts

Filling a 90-day supply usually costs less per pill than three 30-day refills because of reduced dispensing fees. Mail-order pharmacies often add an extra discount.

Pharmacy location

Cash prices for the identical medication can vary 2-5x between neighboring pharmacies. Big-box and warehouse pharmacies are typically lower than retail chains.

Patient assistance & copay programs

  • Manufacturer copay card — eligible commercially-insured patients may pay as little as $0-$25 per fill.
  • Patient assistance program (PAP) — free or low-cost medication for uninsured/under-insured patients meeting income criteria.
  • NeedyMeds & RxAssist — independent directories of available assistance programs.
  • State pharmaceutical assistance programs — many states offer subsidies for seniors and low-income residents.
