Drug Side Effects A-Z

Side effects are unintended physical or psychological effects that occur with a medication. Most are mild and resolve on their own, but some can be serious and require medical attention. Use this hub to look up side effects by drug, by symptom name, or by category.

Side effect categories

Serious side effects

Effects that can be life-threatening or cause lasting harm and require immediate medical attention. Examples include severe bleeding, liver damage, irregular heartbeats, seizures, and suicidal thoughts.

  • Severe bleeding
  • Liver damage
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Seizures

Allergic reactions

Immune-mediated responses ranging from mild rash to life-threatening anaphylaxis. Stop the medication and get emergency care for swelling of the face or throat, difficulty breathing, or widespread hives.

  • Rash and itching
  • Swelling (angioedema)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Anaphylaxis

Long-term effects

Effects that develop or persist after weeks, months, or years of use. These may include dependence, organ-system changes, weight changes, or cognitive effects, and should be monitored by your prescriber.

  • Weight gain or loss
  • Mood changes
  • Hair loss
  • High blood pressure

Common, mild effects

Frequently reported and usually self-limiting. They often improve as the body adjusts to a medication in the first one to two weeks.

  • Nausea
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
