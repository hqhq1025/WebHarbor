Overview

{{ drug.side_effects }}

Common side effects (greater than 10%)

  • Upset stomach, heartburn, or nausea
  • Mild headache or dizziness
  • Drowsiness or fatigue
  • Mild rash or itching
  • Constipation or diarrhea

Less common side effects (1-10%)

  • Gastrointestinal: abdominal pain, gas, bloating, vomiting
  • Cardiovascular: mild fluid retention, swelling of the hands or feet, elevated blood pressure
  • Renal: decreased urine output, increased serum creatinine
  • Nervous system: nervousness, ringing in the ears (tinnitus)
  • Hematologic: easy bruising

Rare but serious side effects (less than 1%)

  • Severe gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation
  • Heart attack, stroke, or blood clots
  • Acute kidney injury or kidney failure
  • Severe allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis
  • Stevens-Johnson syndrome / toxic epidermal necrolysis
  • Hepatic failure or severe liver injury
  • Aseptic meningitis

When to seek emergency help

Get emergency medical help immediately if you experience any of the following:

  • Signs of an allergic reaction: hives, difficult breathing, swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat
  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, slurred speech, or weakness on one side of the body
  • Black, tarry, or bloody stools; coughing up blood or vomit that looks like coffee grounds
  • Severe stomach pain that does not go away
  • Little or no urination; swelling, rapid weight gain
  • Skin rash, blistering, or peeling, especially with fever
