Serious side effects — seek emergency help

Get emergency medical help immediately if you experience any of the following while taking {{ drug.generic_name|capitalize }}:

  • Signs of an allergic reaction: hives, difficult breathing, swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat
  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, slurred speech, or weakness on one side of the body
  • Black, tarry, or bloody stools; coughing up blood or vomit that looks like coffee grounds
  • Severe stomach pain that does not go away
  • Little or no urination; swelling, rapid weight gain
  • Skin rash, blistering, or peeling, especially with fever
  • Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice); dark urine; severe upper-stomach pain spreading to your back

When to call your doctor

Call your healthcare provider if any of the side effects listed above persist, worsen, or interfere with daily activities. Some side effects may resolve on their own as your body adjusts; others may require a dose change or switch to a different medication.

  • Side effects that last more than a few days after starting therapy
  • Any new or unusual symptom not listed on this page
  • Symptoms that interfere with sleep, work, or daily activities
  • Side effects that worsen with each dose

Report side effects to the FDA

You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Reporting helps identify safety issues that may not have been seen in clinical trials.

FDA MedWatch — Adverse Event Reporting Program

  • Online: www.fda.gov/medwatch
  • Phone: 1-800-FDA-1088 (1-800-332-1088)
  • Fax: 1-800-FDA-0178 (1-800-332-0178)
  • Mail: MedWatch, FDA, 5600 Fishers Lane, Rockville, MD 20857

Use FDA Form 3500 for consumer / patient reports and Form 3500A for mandatory reports from manufacturers and importers. Reports may be submitted anonymously.

You may also report adverse events to the manufacturer of your specific brand of {{ drug.generic_name|capitalize }}, who is required to forward the report to the FDA.
