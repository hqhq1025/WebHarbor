Gastrointestinal adverse events

The most frequently reported adverse effects were gastrointestinal in nature and included nausea, vomiting, flatulence, and diarrhea. Peptic ulceration, perforation, or gastrointestinal bleeding has occurred rarely and may be severe.

Cardiovascular

NSAIDs may cause an increased risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. This risk may increase with duration of use.

Renal

Renal papillary necrosis and other renal injury have been reported with long-term use. Acute interstitial nephritis with hematuria, proteinuria, and occasionally nephrotic syndrome has been reported.

Hepatic

Borderline elevations of one or more liver enzymes may occur in up to 15% of patients. Severe hepatic reactions including jaundice and cases of fatal fulminant hepatitis have been reported.

Nervous system

Aseptic meningitis with fever and coma has been observed on rare occasions in patients on ibuprofen therapy. Cognitive dysfunction has been reported in elderly patients.

Report side effects to the FDA

You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Reporting helps identify safety issues that may not have been seen in clinical trials.

FDA MedWatch — Adverse Event Reporting Program

  • Online: www.fda.gov/medwatch
  • Phone: 1-800-FDA-1088 (1-800-332-1088)
  • Fax: 1-800-FDA-0178 (1-800-332-0178)
  • Mail: MedWatch, FDA, 5600 Fishers Lane, Rockville, MD 20857

Use FDA Form 3500 for consumer / patient reports and Form 3500A for mandatory reports from manufacturers and importers. Reports may be submitted anonymously.

You may also report adverse events to the manufacturer of your specific brand of {{ drug.generic_name|capitalize }}, who is required to forward the report to the FDA.
