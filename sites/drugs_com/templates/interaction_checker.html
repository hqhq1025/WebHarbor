Drug Interaction FAQs

What is a drug interaction?

A drug interaction occurs when a medication's effect is altered by another drug, food, beverage, or supplement. Interactions can make a medication less effective, increase side effects, or trigger unexpected reactions.

How do I use the drug interaction checker?

Type the name of a drug in the search box and click Add. Repeat for each medication, supplement, or substance you want to check. Once at least two drugs have been added, click Check Interactions to view the results.

What are the most common drug interactions?

Common interactions include warfarin with NSAIDs, MAO inhibitors with SSRIs, ACE inhibitors with potassium supplements, and statins with certain antibiotics or grapefruit juice.

What foods or drinks interact with my medications?

Grapefruit juice, leafy greens high in vitamin K, dairy products, caffeine, and alcohol can all interact with various prescription drugs. Always check the label and consult your pharmacist.

What happens if I have a drug interaction?

Outcomes range from mild side effects (nausea, drowsiness) to severe events (bleeding, organ damage, serotonin syndrome). The severity depends on the specific drugs and your individual health profile.

Can supplements interact with drugs?

Yes. Herbal supplements such as St. John's wort, ginkgo, and garlic, as well as vitamins and minerals, can meaningfully alter the action of prescription drugs.

Are drug interactions always dangerous?

No. Many interactions are minor and clinically insignificant. Some are even beneficial and are used intentionally in combination therapy. Your healthcare provider can help interpret severity.

How serious are drug interactions?

Severity is typically classified as major (avoid combination), moderate (use with caution and monitoring), or minor (limited clinical effect). The checker labels each interaction accordingly.

What should I do if my drugs interact?

Do not stop taking any prescribed medication on your own. Contact your doctor or pharmacist promptly to discuss whether a dosage adjustment, timing change, or alternative therapy is appropriate.
