Seek immediate medical help

Call your doctor or emergency services right away if you experience any of the following while taking {{ drug.generic_name }}:

  • Signs of a severe allergic reaction: hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, difficulty breathing.
  • Chest pain, irregular heartbeat, fainting, or sudden severe headache.
  • Unusual bleeding or bruising, black or bloody stools, vomiting blood.
  • Severe skin reaction: blistering, peeling, or a red/purple rash that spreads.
  • Confusion, slurred speech, seizures, or loss of consciousness.

In the U.S., dial 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Outside the U.S., contact your local emergency number.

General precautions

  • Tell your doctor about all medical conditions, including kidney, liver, or heart problems.
  • Disclose all medications you take, including prescription drugs, over-the-counter products, vitamins, and herbal supplements.
  • Inform your doctor if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
  • Do not stop taking {{ drug.generic_name }} abruptly unless directed by your prescriber.
  • Store the medication as directed and keep out of reach of children.
