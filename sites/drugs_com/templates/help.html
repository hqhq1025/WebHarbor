Frequently Asked Questions

How do I search for a drug?

Type the brand or generic drug name into the search box at the top of any page. As you type, suggestions appear with matching drugs and conditions. You can also browse alphabetically via the Drugs A-Z page, or use the A-Z strip in the header.

What is the drug interaction checker?

The Drug Interaction Checker lets you enter two or more medications and reports known interactions classified by severity (major, moderate, minor). It is an educational tool and does not replace pharmacist or physician review.

How do I use the pill identifier?

Open the Pill Identifier and enter any combination of imprint code, shape, and color from the pill. The tool returns matching tablet or capsule images along with the drug name and dosage form.

Can I trust Drugs.com information?

Our drug monographs are written by licensed pharmacists and reviewed by physicians. Each page lists the medical reviewer and review date. Information should always be confirmed with your healthcare provider, especially for dosing and individual contraindications.

How do I report a side effect?

Suspected adverse drug reactions should be reported to the FDA MedWatch program at 1-800-FDA-1088 or via fda.gov/medwatch. You may also send corrections or comments about our content via our Contact page.
