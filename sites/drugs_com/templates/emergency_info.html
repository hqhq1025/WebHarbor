Emergency Drug Information

In a life-threatening emergency, call 911 immediately.

Poison Control Center

1-800-222-1222

National Poison Control Hotline — available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
For poisoning emergencies only. Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies.

The Poison Help hotline provides immediate advice on managing poisoning situations. Keep this number accessible at home and in your phone.

Drug Overdose

If you suspect a drug overdose:

  1. Call 911 or your local emergency number immediately if the person is unconscious, not breathing, or having seizures.
  2. Call Poison Control (1-800-222-1222) if the person is awake and able to communicate.
  3. Try to identify the drug, amount taken, and when it was taken.
  4. Do not leave the person alone.
  5. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by emergency services.

Allergic Reactions (Anaphylaxis)

Signs of a severe allergic reaction include:

  • Difficulty breathing, wheezing, or throat tightening
  • Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
  • Rapid heartbeat or drop in blood pressure
  • Skin rash, hives, or itching over large areas of the body
  • Dizziness, fainting, or loss of consciousness

Call 911 immediately if you or someone else is experiencing these symptoms after taking a medication. If an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) is available, use it right away and then call 911.

Report a Side Effect to the FDA

You can report serious side effects or unexpected drug reactions to the FDA through the MedWatch program:

FDA MedWatch — Adverse Event Reporting Program

  • Online: www.fda.gov/medwatch
  • Phone: 1-800-FDA-1088 (1-800-332-1088)
  • Fax: 1-800-FDA-0178 (1-800-332-0178)
  • Mail: MedWatch, FDA, 5600 Fishers Lane, Rockville, MD 20857

Use FDA Form 3500 for consumer reports and Form 3500A for mandatory reports from manufacturers. Reports may be submitted anonymously.

Drug Interaction Emergencies

If you are concerned about a dangerous drug interaction you or someone else may be experiencing:

  • Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
  • Use the Drug Interaction Checker to identify potential interactions before taking medications together.

Find Emergency Pharmacies

Many pharmacies offer 24-hour emergency services. Check with your local pharmacy chain or hospital pharmacy for after-hours access to critical medications.
