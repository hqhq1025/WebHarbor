Before taking this medicine

Important: Tell your healthcare provider about all medications you're taking before starting {{ drug.generic_name }}.

Before taking {{ drug.generic_name }}, tell your doctor if you:

  • Have allergies — You should not use {{ drug.generic_name }} if you are allergic to it or any of its ingredients. Tell your doctor about all known drug or food allergies.
  • Take other medications — Many drugs can interact with {{ drug.generic_name }}. Share a complete list of prescription medicines, over-the-counter products, vitamins, and herbal supplements with your healthcare provider. Check for drug interactions ›
  • Have medical conditions — Tell your doctor if you have ever had heart disease, high blood pressure, heart attack, stroke, blood clots, stomach ulcers or bleeding, liver or kidney disease, asthma, or other chronic conditions that may affect how {{ drug.generic_name }} works for you.
  • Are pregnant or breastfeeding — Ask a doctor before using this medicine if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding. See the pregnancy and lactation information for {{ drug.generic_name }} (pregnancy risk: {{ drug.pregnancy_risk }}).

What happens if I miss a dose?

Since {{ drug.generic_name }} is often used when needed, you may not be on a dosing schedule. If you are on a schedule, take the missed dose as soon as you remember. Skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next scheduled dose. Do not take extra medicine to make up the missed dose.

What happens if I overdose?

Seek emergency medical attention or call the Poison Help line at 1-800-222-1222.

Alcohol & food interactions

Avoid drinking alcohol while taking {{ drug.generic_name }}. Alcohol may increase the risk of certain side effects, including drowsiness, stomach bleeding, or liver damage. Ask your doctor or pharmacist before consuming grapefruit or grapefruit juice, as it can interact with several medications.

Lab test interactions

{{ drug.generic_name|capitalize }} may interfere with certain laboratory tests, possibly causing false results. Tell any healthcare provider who treats you or orders lab work that you are using this medicine.
