Frequently asked questions

Are GitHub Skills courses really free?

Yes. Every course on this page is free to take, even if you only have a free GitHub account. There are no premium tiers, no upsells, and no credit-card prompts. The course content itself is open-source — most repos accept community pull requests for translations, typo fixes, and new exercises.

How do I earn a certificate?

Each course ends with an automatic completion check that runs in your course repository. Once all steps pass, the Skills bot opens a final pull request that adds a badge to your profile and emails you a downloadable PDF certificate. Certificates include the course name, your GitHub handle, and the completion date — all verifiable from the course's public commit history.

Can I take courses at my own pace?

Absolutely. Skills courses don't expire, don't have cohort start dates, and don't time out between sessions. Your progress lives in your own copy of the course repo, so you can pause for a day, a week, or a year and pick up exactly where you left off — same branch, same open PR.

Are courses available offline?

The interactive grader needs to talk to GitHub.com to score your pushes, so you'll need a connection for the auto-feedback loop. However, every course's reading material is plain Markdown in the public repo — clone it once and you can read every lesson on a flight without WiFi.

Do Skills courses count for CPE / continuing-education credits?

Skills certificates are recognized by several professional bodies — including ISC2 and CompTIA — for self-reported continuing professional education (CPE) credits. Each completed course is roughly 1 CPE per hour of content. Check your certifying body's policy for the exact mapping.

I'm an instructor — can I use Skills in my classroom?

Yes, and we'd love that. GitHub Education partners with thousands of universities and bootcamps. Sign up for a free Teacher account to get classroom dashboards, autograding, and the ability to assign Skills courses as homework with one click.
