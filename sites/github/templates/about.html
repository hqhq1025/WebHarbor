Our mission

To accelerate human progress through developer collaboration. Software is rewriting every industry on the planet, and the people who build it — developers — are the most important profession of our time. Our job is to give them the best place to do that work, and to keep that place open, accessible, and welcoming to the next billion people who learn to code.

Sixteen years of building together

A few of the moments along the way.

2008

Founded in San Francisco

Tom Preston-Werner, Chris Wanstrath, and PJ Hyett launch GitHub.com — a hosted Git platform with a social layer.

2012

10 million repositories

GitHub crosses 10M repos and becomes the de facto home of open-source. Pull requests reshape how teams ship.

2018

Joined Microsoft

Microsoft acquires GitHub for $7.5B and commits to running it as an independent, developer-first platform.

2019

GitHub Actions launches

CI/CD becomes a first-class part of GitHub. Free for public repos. The Marketplace fills with 10,000 actions in a year.

2021

GitHub Copilot

The world's first AI pair programmer enters technical preview, then ships GA in 2022. By 2024 it's used by 1.8M paying developers.

2024

100M developers

The platform crosses 100M developer accounts — projected to reach 200M by 2030, with India and Latin America leading growth.

What we value

Four ideas you'll see show up in every product decision, every code review, and every all-hands.

🎯

Customer-obsessed

We measure ourselves by what developers ship — not by what we ship to them. Every feature is a hypothesis until a customer tells us it's a habit.

💡

Ship to learn

Small experiments in production beat large debates in conference rooms. Our internal motto: "What would you ship if you could ship it tomorrow?"

🌍

Distributed by default

We were remote before remote was cool. Hubbers in 80+ countries means we design products for asynchronous, global, multilingual teams — because we are one.

🔓

Open by default

The platform that hosts open-source must itself be open. We publish our roadmap, our incident reports, and our architectural decisions in public.

Home of open source

GitHub hosts the largest open-source community on Earth — Linux, Kubernetes, React, TensorFlow, VS Code, PyTorch, Rust, and the rest of the modern stack. We sponsor, fund, and protect that ecosystem through GitHub Sponsors, the GitHub Fund, and free unlimited Actions and Codespaces minutes for every public repository.
