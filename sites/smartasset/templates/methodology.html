SmartAsset Editorial Methodology

How we rate products

Every brokerage, bank, lender, robo-advisor and insurance carrier on SmartAsset is rated on a 1.0–5.0 scale using a four-factor model:

  1. Cost (30%). Headline fees, expense ratios, account minimums, plus an estimated 5-year all-in cost for a representative $50,000 portfolio.
  2. Product depth (25%). Account types, asset coverage, cash-management features, and integrations with tax software.
  3. Customer experience (25%). Time-to-respond on support tickets, app store ratings (weighted by review count), and dispute resolution outcomes.
  4. Safety & transparency (20%). Regulatory record, FDIC / SIPC coverage, conflicts of interest disclosed, and the clarity of the fee schedule.

How we evaluate financial advisors

SmartAdvisor only refers to advisors who are:

  • Registered with either the SEC or a state regulator.
  • Operating under a fiduciary standard at all times.
  • Have a clean compliance record on their Form ADV (no felonies, no client-fund misappropriation, no settled regulatory actions in the past 10 years).
  • Hold one of: CFP®, CFA, ChFC, CPA/PFS.
  • Manage a minimum of $50M in client assets.

How our calculators work

Every SmartAsset calculator is server-side, deterministic, and open-source-by-design. The math behind each calculator is documented in its "How this is calculated" sidebar and uses IRS / FRBNY published values for tax tables, federal rate quotes, and inflation indices.

How we update content

SmartReads articles list a "last updated" timestamp at the top. Tax-related articles are reviewed quarterly and within 30 days of any Treasury / IRS rulemaking that affects the calculation.

Conflicts of interest

SmartAsset earns referral fees from advisors who join the SmartAdvisor Match program. Editorial coverage of products is fully independent of those relationships, and we do not accept payment for review placement or favorable ratings.
