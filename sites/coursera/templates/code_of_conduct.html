Coursera Community Code of Conduct

Last revised: February 18, 2026

Coursera serves learners of every age, background, and identity. The behaviors below help make Coursera safe and productive for everyone.

Behaviors we expect

  • Treat fellow learners and mentors with respect.
  • Give and accept constructive feedback graciously.
  • Keep peer-review feedback specific, kind, and focused on the work.
  • Welcome new learners and answer questions you can answer.

Behaviors that are not tolerated

  • Harassment, hate speech, or threats of violence.
  • Doxxing — publishing or threatening to publish private information.
  • Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, or disability.
  • Spam, scams, and undisclosed promotion of paid services.
  • Posting sexually explicit or violent imagery in any forum or peer-review submission.

Reporting violations

Use the "Report" link on any forum post or peer review. Urgent safety concerns can be escalated to trust-safety@coursera.org; reports are reviewed within 48 hours.

Enforcement

  1. Warning — first or borderline issue. Educational reminder.
  2. Forum suspension — repeat or escalating issue. 7–30 days.
  3. Account termination — severe or unaddressed pattern. Loss of certificates and access.
